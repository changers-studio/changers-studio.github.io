Our work process

Full Interior Design Project

Design project this is not just a visualization of your future interior.

It is a comprehensive solution that includes layout planning, selection of materials, colors, and furniture, as well as technical drawings for implementation.

Main goal is

to transform the space into a comfortable, harmonious, and stylish environment that reflects your needs and budget.

Design process

01 Brief and needs analysis

01 Brief and needs analysis

We start with a conversation about your preferences, style, and budget. You fill out a short brief, and we already know what matters most to you.

We analyze your lifestyle: how you use the space, what colors you like, and whether any family changes are planned.

We take measurements, analyze the technical conditions, and create the foundation for the future project. This ensures that the interior will be not only beautiful but also perfectly functional for you.

02 Layout solution

02 Layout solution

You receive a plan in 2D and 3D formats. At this stage, the proportions and functionality of the space are visible—without details, but with the ergonomics already in place.

We offer several options for the layout of zones and furniture. We take into account the lighting, flow of movement, and your lifestyle.

This is the moment when you see how comfortable and logical the space can become. The chosen option becomes the foundation for the entire project.

03 Concept and style

03 Concept and style

We present our vision in the form of a mood board—colors, materials, furniture, and lighting.

This is a visual “atmosphere” of the future interior. You can see the color palette, textures, materials, as well as the suggested furniture and lighting.

The mood board helps you feel the style and understand how everything works together. This is the stage when the interior begins to “come to life.

04 3D-visualization

04 3D-visualization

Realistic images that look like photos of the future. You can see the interior “in real life” even before the renovation begins.

This is an accurate visualization of colors, furniture, lighting, and materials. You immediately understand how the space will look in reality and can even feel its vibe.

At this stage, it’s easy to make changes so that the result fully meets your expectations.

05 Working documentation

05 Working documentation

A complete set of drawings and schedules for the craftsmen—everything needed for precise execution of the interior.

We prepare drawings for built-in furniture, electrical systems, plumbing, lighting, and finishes.

In addition, we provide specification tables for furniture, lighting, and materials — including quantities and links to manufacturers. This saves you time and eliminates errors during the renovation.

06 Author supervision (optional)

06 Author supervision (optional)

We oversee the renovation, assist with material selection, and monitor the quality of work.

We help select furniture, lighting, and finishes. If necessary, we make adjustments to the drawings to adapt them to real conditions.

This relieves you of the hassle and ensures the result matches what you saw in the visualizations.

Typical composition of a design project:

  • 01 Measured plan
  • 02 Demolition and installation plan
  • 03 Furniture layout plan
  • 04 Ceiling, floor, skirting, and wall finishes plan
  • 05 Layout plan for plumbing, lighting, outlets, and switches
  • 06 Tile and flooring layout diagrams
  • 07 Custom furniture drawings
  • 08 Specifications for furniture, lighting, plumbing, and materials
  • 09 Visualizations and 3D diagrams

Timeline for the design project:

The overall duration depends on the number of spaces and the complexity of the task.

On average:

  • 01
    5–10 business days for each individual space (depending on its size).
  • 02
    +3–4 days for making adjustments to each room (if needed).
  • 03
    + approximately 2 working weeks for preparing the construction drawings.

For example:

  • 01
    A design project for a three-room apartment takes approximately 8–10 weeks.
  • 02
    A design project for a restaurant with an area of 100 m² takes approximately 10–12 weeks.

The total project duration may vary depending on the size and complexity of the space and is always confirmed individually.

Conceptual Design Project

For those who want professional space planning and a visual concept without a full package of technical drawings.

We develop the optimal layout, create moodboards with carefully selected furniture, materials, and lighting — and prepare a basic set of drawings required to start implementation.

It’s a convenient format for new builds, apartments under renovation, or clients planning to execute the project independently.

What’s included

  • 01 Development of 2–3 layout options for the space
  • 02 Coordination and selection of the best solution
  • 03 Creation of moodboards for each room — with color palette, textures, furniture, and lighting
  • 04 A minimal set of drawings:

    furniture plan;

    demolition and installation plan;

    electrical plan (sockets, switches, lighting outputs).

  • 05 Tables with selected furniture, lighting, and materials

Result

A ready functional layout, visual concept, and technical foundation for independent implementation or contractor handover.

Timeline

  • 01
    7 working days for space planning;
  • 02
    10 working days for the concept development (moodboards, materials palette, furniture and lighting selection);
  • 03
    +7 working days for preparing the basic technical documentation package (furniture, electrical, and demolition/installation plans, plus specification tables for furniture, materials, and lighting).

The total project duration may vary depending on the size and complexity of the space and is always confirmed individually.

Benefits

  • 01
    Fast delivery of a complete concept. You get a full picture of the interior without long waiting times.
  • 02
    Optimized costs. You don’t pay for a full technical package if you plan to manage part of the work independently.
  • 03
    Rational and structured approach. Several layout options allow you to find the most ergonomic and logical solution.
  • 04
    Visual clarity. Moodboards help you see the style, materials, and overall atmosphere of the space.
  • 05
    Ready for execution. The basic documentation and furniture/material tables let you start the realization right away.
